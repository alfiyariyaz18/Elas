Wills and Estate Planning Services

In the UAE, wills and succession planning are crucial for ensuring that your assets are distributed according to your wishes after your death. The UAE follows Sharia law for inheritance, but non-Muslim expatriates have the option to register a will to distribute their assets according to their preferences. Without a will, the local courts will apply Sharia principles, which may not align with your wishes, particularly regarding the distribution of assets among family members.

Succession planning in the UAE involves drafting a will, considering assets in multiple jurisdictions, and understanding the legal implications of your decisions. It's advisable to register your will with the relevant authorities, such as the DIFC Wills Service Centre, to ensure it is legally recognized.

Given the complexities of inheritance laws in the UAE, it is essential to consult with legal professionals to create a comprehensive succession plan that reflects your wishes and protects your family's future. Proper planning helps avoid legal disputes and ensures a smooth transition of assets.
